# Design

The website should feel like a restrained corporate technology site: clear,
credible, and practical. Avoid marketing clutter, oversized claims, and
decorative UI that does not help the visitor understand the company.

## Content Direction

XeonTek is a self-funded product company. The public site should explain that it
builds and operates its own AI-driven platforms for property analytics,
investment intelligence, and financial modelling.

Primary audiences:

- Potential hires.
- Partners and vendors.
- Regulators or due-diligence reviewers.
- Readers arriving from whitepapers or search.

Tone:

- Use first-person plural where useful.
- Prefer specific, plain language.
- Avoid sales CTAs, exaggerated AI claims, and generic startup wording.

## Page Roles

- Homepage: explain what XeonTek is and what it builds.
- About: company story, operating model, and values.
- Research: whitepapers and published thinking.
- Careers: current roles, if hiring.
- Contact: factual contact route, not a sales funnel.
- Security: security posture and vulnerability contact.
- Privacy and Terms: legal website notices.

## Visual Direction

- Typography should feel editorial and legible.
- Use generous whitespace and strong hierarchy.
- Keep surfaces mostly white or off-white with slate text.
- Use teal as an accent for links, active states, and small highlights.
- Avoid large teal fills, heavy gradients, and decorative background effects.
- Cards should be simple, flat, and purposeful.

## Interaction And Accessibility

- Content must remain visible without JavaScript.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Keep focus states visible on links, buttons, navigation, and form fields.
- Use semantic page structure and accessible dialog titles.
- Keep color contrast strong, especially for muted text and accent links.
